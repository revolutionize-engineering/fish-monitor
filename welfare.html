Major Challenges For Behaviour Analysis Tools Today

Manual inspection is a lot of work

Inspection takes time and gives a limited view.

Huge number of individuals

Cages typically have 100 000 to 250 000 fish in them, making it impossible to monitor the whole population by traditional means.

Limited information from cameras

Cage coverage using cameras alone is very small. Fish naturally migrate up and down in the water column, meaning it is hard to get a representative measurement without covering a larger volume. Periods of poor visibility, low light or darkness increase the challenge.

Many behavior patterns are impossible to detect

Fish density, movement, and distribution are impossible to detect with cameras.

monitor stressors affecting your fish

In order to detect behavioral anomalies we must know what “normal” looks like. Bluegrove has built a database of over 30000 cage days of salmon behavior. With Welfare Shield:

  • You can see the fish reacting to stressful event in real time.
  • High stress events also remain visible on historical graphs

Assess if your fish have recovered from delicing

See the difference after delicing. For example:

  • Prior to delicing, fish were staying deep during the day when not eating.
  • After delicing many fish chose to stay near the surface all day, while others behaved more normally, suggesting some are slower to recover than others.

Case study

farm in Norway sees fish behaviour changes; first due to tuna in the
cage, and later after delicing.

Analysis based on fish positioning data over a 3 month period. The graph shows the deviation in day time swimming depth relative to the mean for the past month. A value close to zero, means little deviation, while a negative value (higher on y axis) indicates that the fish are swimming closer to the surface than usual. Likewise, negative values mean the fish are swimming deeper than they have been previously
